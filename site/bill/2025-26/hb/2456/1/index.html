HB 2456 - Juvenile firearm interv. ¶

Source

Section 1 ¶

  1. A juvenile firearm early intervention alternative is an alternate method of resolving cases where a person is charged in juvenile court with unlawful possession of a firearm under RCW 9.41.040(2)(a)(v). For a person to qualify for a juvenile firearm early intervention alternative under this section:

    1. The person must be charged in juvenile court with unlawful possession of a firearm under RCW 9.41.040(2)(a)(v);

    2. Within two weeks of the date charges are filed and upon agreement of the prosecutor the person must enter into an agreement with the prosecutor to pursue the juvenile firearm early intervention alternative in lieu of prosecution.

  2. A juvenile firearm early intervention alternative must consist of the following:

    1. A period of intensive community supervision assigned to a juvenile probation counselor with a reduced caseload for 12 months which may include a period extending beyond the 21st birthday of the participant;

    2. The participant complying with all conditions of release and supervision, which may include:

      1. Curfew;

      2. Geographic restrictions;

      3. Restrictions on associations with certain people;

      4. Electronic monitoring;

    3. Abstaining from drugs and alcohol;

    1. School attendance; and

    2. Any other conditions deemed appropriate by the court or the juvenile probation officer to ensure the participant's success and the safety of the public;

    3. The participant agreeing to waive their right to a speedy trial;

    4. The participant signing a consent to allow for random, suspicionless searches of their person, vehicle, residence, and personal property during the period of supervision in order to allow the juvenile probation officer to search for firearms, firearm parts, ammunition, illegal substances, or other items that would constitute a violation of state law or the terms of the alternative;

    1. The participant agreeing that the juvenile probation officer has the authority to arrest the participant, or cause the arrest of the participant without a warrant when the probation officer has probable cause to believe the participant has violated a condition of the alternative and there is a risk to public safety or to the health and welfare of the participant, pending a court hearing that considers the nature of the alleged violation or violations;

    2. The participant successfully engaging in the following mandatory services:

      1. Multisystemic therapy; and

      2. A credible messenger or mentorship program; and

    3. The participant successfully engaging in additional appropriate services, identified by juvenile probation, which may include the following:

      1. Completion of a mental health evaluation and compliance with any treatment recommendations;

      2. Completion of a substance use disorder evaluation and compliance with any treatment recommendations;

      3. Completion of an evaluation with a physician or advanced registered nurse practitioner and compliance with any medications prescribed to treat any mental health or substance use disorders; and

      4. Compliance with substance use monitoring through urinalysis, an alcohol detection breathalyzer device, a transdermal sensor device, or other technology designed to detect drugs or alcohol in a person's system.

  3. During the period of the alternative, at least every three months, the court must hold a hearing, with the defendant present, to consider status reports from the juvenile probation officer detailing the participant's compliance with conditions of the alternative.

  4. If the participant successfully completes the alternative, the charge must be dismissed.

  5. If it appears to the prosecuting attorney that the participant is not substantially complying with all alternative conditions as reflected by a written update, the prosecuting attorney may make a motion for termination of the alternative.

    1. After notice is provided to the participant, the court shall hold a hearing to determine whether the alternative should be terminated.

    2. Before the hearing to determine whether the alternative should be terminated, the participant and their attorney must be advised of the nature of the alleged noncompliance and provided discovery of evidence supporting the motion, including names and contact information of witnesses.

    3. During the hearing to determine whether the alternative should be terminated, the court must consider the following factors:

      1. The nature of the alleged noncompliance; and

      2. Any aggravating or mitigating circumstances.

    4. If the court finds the participant is not substantially complying with the conditions of the alternative, the court shall provide in its written order the grounds for termination of the alternative.

  6. For purposes of this section, "alternative" means the juvenile firearm early intervention alternative.

Section 2 ¶

  1. The chief of police of a municipality or the sheriff of a county shall within thirty days after the filing of an application of any person, issue a license to such person to carry a pistol concealed on his or her person within this state for five years from date of issue, for the purposes of protection or while engaged in business, sport, or while traveling. However, if the applicant does not have a valid permanent Washington driver's license or Washington state identification card or has not been a resident of the state for the previous consecutive ninety days, the issuing authority shall have up to sixty days after the filing of the application to issue a license. The issuing authority shall not refuse to accept completed applications for concealed pistol licenses during regular business hours.

The applicant's constitutional right to bear arms shall not be denied, unless:

a. He or she is ineligible to possess a firearm under the provisions of RCW 9.41.040 or 9.41.045, or is prohibited from possessing a firearm under federal law;

b. The applicant's concealed pistol license is in a revoked status;

c. He or she is under twenty-one years of age;

d. He or she is subject to a court order or injunction regarding firearms pursuant to chapter 7.105 RCW, or RCW 9A.46.080, 10.99.040, 10.99.045, 26.09.050, 26.09.060, 26.10.040, 26.26B.020, or 26.26A.470, or any of the former RCW 10.14.080, 26.10.115, 26.50.060, and 26.50.070;

e. He or she is free on bond or personal recognizance pending trial, appeal, or sentencing for a felony offense;

f. He or she has an outstanding warrant for his or her arrest from any court of competent jurisdiction for a felony or misdemeanor; or

g. He or she has been ordered to forfeit a firearm under RCW 9.41.098(1)(e) within one year before filing an application to carry a pistol concealed on his or her person.

No person convicted of a felony may have his or her right to possess firearms restored or his or her privilege to carry a concealed pistol restored, unless the person has been granted relief from disabilities by the attorney general under 18 U.S.C. Sec. 925(c), or RCW 9.41.040 (3) or (4) applies.

  1. [Empty]

    1. The issuing authority shall conduct a check through the national instant criminal background check system, the Washington state patrol electronic database, the health care authority electronic database, and with other agencies or resources as appropriate, to determine whether the applicant is ineligible under RCW 9.41.040 or 9.41.045 to possess a firearm, or is prohibited from possessing a firearm under federal law, and therefore ineligible for a concealed pistol license.

    2. The issuing authority shall deny a permit to anyone who is found to be prohibited from possessing a firearm under federal or state law.

    3. (a) and (b) of this subsection apply whether the applicant is applying for a new concealed pistol license or to renew a concealed pistol license.

    4. A background check for an original license must be conducted through the Washington state patrol criminal identification section and shall include a national check from the federal bureau of investigation through the submission of fingerprints. The results will be returned to the issuing authority. The applicant may request and receive a copy of the results of the background check from the issuing authority. If the applicant seeks to amend or correct their record, the applicant must contact the Washington state patrol for a Washington state record or the federal bureau of investigation for records from other jurisdictions.

  2. Any person whose firearms rights have been restricted and who has been granted relief from disabilities by the attorney general under 18 U.S.C. Sec. 925(c) or who is exempt under 18 U.S.C. Sec. 921(a)(20)(A) shall have his or her right to acquire, receive, transfer, ship, transport, carry, and possess firearms in accordance with Washington state law restored except as otherwise prohibited by this chapter.

  3. A signed application for a concealed pistol license shall constitute a waiver of confidentiality and written request that the health care authority, mental health institutions, and other health care facilities release information relevant to the applicant's eligibility for a concealed pistol license to an inquiring court or law enforcement agency.

The application for an original license shall include a complete set of fingerprints to be forwarded to the Washington state patrol.

The license and application shall contain a warning substantially as follows:

CAUTION: Although state and local laws do not differ, federal law and state law on the possession of firearms differ. If you are prohibited by federal law from possessing a firearm, you may be prosecuted in federal court. A state license is not a defense to a federal prosecution.

The license shall contain a description of the major differences between state and federal law and an explanation of the fact that local laws and ordinances on firearms are preempted by state law and must be consistent with state law.

The application shall contain questions about the applicant's eligibility under RCW 9.41.040 and federal law to possess a pistol, the applicant's place of birth, and whether the applicant is a United States citizen. If the applicant is not a United States citizen, the applicant must provide the applicant's country of citizenship, United States issued alien number or admission number, and the basis on which the applicant claims to be exempt from federal prohibitions on firearm possession by aliens. The applicant shall not be required to produce a birth certificate or other evidence of citizenship. A person who is not a citizen of the United States shall, if applicable, meet the additional requirements of RCW 9.41.173 and produce proof of compliance with RCW 9.41.173 upon application. The license may be in triplicate or in a form to be prescribed by the department of licensing.

A photograph of the applicant may be required as part of the application and printed on the face of the license.

The original thereof shall be delivered to the licensee, the duplicate shall within seven days be sent to the director of licensing and the triplicate shall be preserved for six years, by the authority issuing the license.

The department of licensing shall make available to law enforcement and corrections agencies, in an online format, all information received under this subsection.

  2. Revenues to the juvenile firearm early intervention services account consist of:

    1. Moneys deposited in this account pursuant to RCW 9.41.070; and

    2. Funds appropriated by the legislature or other funds from public and private sources.
