Section 1 ¶

The legislature recognizes that with the enactment of Engrossed Substitute Senate Bill No. 5801 during the 2025 legislative session, the legislature addressed the state transportation system's pressing near, mid, and long-term needs that necessitated reliance on reliable funding resources, as well as the efficient use of those resources. Because the production, maintenance, and utilization of transportation resources across the state continues to be an inherently complex, multifaceted issue, the legislature intends with this act to continue to address these resource needs by addressing certain topics from Engrossed Substitute Senate Bill No. 5801, and associated matters, that require additional legislative work.

Section 101 ¶

"Mobile credential" is the electronic extension of the department's normally issued physical version that conveys identity, driving privilege, or both, that resides on a mobile device or requires a mobile device as part of the process to gain access to the related information.

Section 102 ¶

"Relying party" is any federal, state, local, or private entity where a mobile credential may be presented for verification purposes. Relying parties shall authenticate an electronic credential in accordance with applicable American association of motor vehicle administrators and international organization for standardization standards prior to acceptance of the electronic credential.

Section 103 ¶

  1. If a law requires a person to provide proof of identification, the requirement may be satisfied with a mobile credential so long as the mobile credential is electronically verified. Each governmental agency of the state shall determine whether, and the extent to which, a governmental agency will accept a mobile credential. However, as required under RCW 46.20.017, a licensee must have the licensee's physical credential on the licensee's person while operating a motor vehicle.

  2. Relying parties shall only retain data from a mobile credential for which the relying party explicitly obtained consent from the credential holder. Relying parties must inform the credential holder of the use and retention period of the mobile credential data.

Section 503 ¶

  1. The department shall establish a process for issuing a lease for the use of the right-of-way by a clean energy facility developer. Consistent with federal highway administration approval, the lease must include the right of direct ingress and egress from the highway for construction and maintenance of the clean energy facility during nonpeak hours if public safety is not adversely affected. Direct ingress and egress may be allowed at any time for the construction of the facility if public safety is not adversely affected and if construction will not substantially interfere with traffic flow during peak traffic periods. The lease may specify an indirect ingress and egress to the facility if it is reasonable and available for the particular location.

  2. The cost of the lease must be limited to the fair market value of the portion of the right-of-way being used by the clean energy facility developer and the direct administrative expenses incurred by the department in processing the lease application.

If the department and the clean energy facility developer are unable to agree on the cost of the lease, the clean energy facility developer may submit the cost of the lease to binding arbitration by serving written notice on the department. Within 30 days of receiving the notice, each party shall furnish a list of acceptable arbitrators. The parties shall select an arbitrator; failing to agree on an arbitrator, each party shall select one arbitrator and the two arbitrators shall select a third arbitrator for an arbitration panel. The arbitrator or panel shall determine the cost of the lease based on comparable siting agreements. Costs of the arbitration, including compensation for the arbitrator's services, must be borne equally by the parties participating in the arbitration and each party shall bear its own costs and expenses, including legal fees and witness expenses, in connection with the arbitration proceeding.

  1. The department shall act on an application for a lease within 60 days of receiving a completed application, unless a clean energy facility developer consents to a different time period.

  2. The reasons for a denial of a lease application must be supported by substantial evidence contained in a written record.

  3. The department may adopt rules to implement this section.

  4. All lease money paid to the department under this section shall be deposited in the motor vehicle fund created in RCW 46.68.070.

  5. This section does not apply when a clean energy facility is procured for delivery under chapter 47.47 RCW or when the clean energy facility developer is the department or contracted by the department.

  6. For the purposes of this section:

    1. "Right-of-way" means all state-owned land within a state highway corridor and any other land under the jurisdiction of the department.

    2. "Clean energy facility developer" means every corporation, company, association, joint stock association, firm, partnership, or person that constructs, owns, operates, or manages any clean energy facility. "Clean energy facility developer" includes a service provider's contractors, subcontractors, and legal successors.

Section 504 ¶

  1. The carbon emissions reduction account is created in the state treasury. Moneys in the account may be spent only after appropriation. Expenditures from the account are intended to affect reductions in transportation sector carbon emissions through a variety of carbon reducing investments. These can include, but are not limited to: Transportation alternatives to single occupancy passenger vehicles; reductions in single occupancy passenger vehicle miles traveled; reductions in per mile emissions in vehicles, including through the funding of alternative fuel infrastructure and incentive programs; and emission reduction programs for freight transportation, including motor vehicles and rail, as well as for ferries and other maritime and port activities. Expenditures from the account may only be made for transportation carbon emission reducing purposes and may not be made for highway purposes authorized under the 18th Amendment of the Washington state Constitution, other than specified in this section, and shall be made in accordance with subsection (2) of this section. It is the legislature's intent that expenditures from the account used to reduce carbon emissions be made with the goal of achieving equity for communities that historically have been omitted or adversely impacted by past transportation policies and practices.

  2. Appropriations in an omnibus transportation appropriations act from the carbon emissions reduction account shall be made exclusively to fund the following activities:

    1. Active transportation;

    2. Transit programs and projects;

    3. Alternative fuel and electrification;

    4. Clean energy facilities located in or on right-of-way or property under the jurisdiction of the department of transportation;

    5. Ferries; and

    6. Rail.
