Engrossed Senate Bill 5135 - Session Law Chapter 330 Year 2021 ¶

Source

Section 1 ¶

This section adds a new section to an existing chapter 4.24. Here is the modified chapter for context.

  1. A person may bring a civil action for damages against any person who knowingly causes a law enforcement officer to arrive at a location to contact another person with the intent to:

    1. Infringe on the other person's rights under the Washington state or United States Constitutions;

    2. Unlawfully discriminate against the other person;

    3. Cause the other person to feel harassed, humiliated, or embarrassed;

    4. Cause the other person to be expelled from a place in which the other person is lawfully located; or

    5. Damage the other person's:

      1. Reputation or standing in the community; or

      2. Financial, economic, consumer, or business prospects or interests.

  2. A person shall not be held liable under subsection (1) of this section if the person acted in good faith in causing a law enforcement officer to arrive.

  3. Upon prevailing in an action under this section, the plaintiff may recover:

    1. The greater of:

      1. Economic and noneconomic damages; or

      2. $250 against each defendant found liable under this section; and

    2. Punitive damages.

  4. The court may award reasonable attorneys' fees and costs to the prevailing plaintiff in an action under this section.

  5. A civil action under this section:

    1. May be maintained in a court of limited jurisdiction if the total damages claimed do not exceed the statutory limit for damages that the court of limited jurisdiction may award; and

    2. Does not affect a right or remedy available under any other law of this state.
