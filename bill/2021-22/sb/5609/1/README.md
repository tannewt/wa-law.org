# Senate Bill 5609

[Source](http://lawfilesext.leg.wa.gov/biennium/2021-22/Xml/Bills/Senate%20Bills/5609.xml)
## Section 1
> This section modifies existing section [10.64.110](/rcw/10_criminal_procedure/10.064_judgments_and_sentences.md). Here is the [modified chapter](rcw/10_criminal_procedure/10.064_judgments_and_sentences.md) for context.

1. Following June 15, 1977, except as provided in subsection (3) of this section, there shall be affixed to the original of every judgment and sentence of a felony conviction in every court in this state  a fingerprint of the defendant  who is the subject of the order. When requested by the clerk of the court, the actual affixing of fingerprints shall be done by a representative of the office of the county sheriff.

2. The clerk of the court shall attest that the fingerprints appearing on the judgment in sentence,  or docket, is that of the individual who is the subject of the judgment or conviction,  or docket entry.

3. Amended judgment and sentences issued pursuant to , No. 96873-0 (Feb. 25, 2021), are exempt from the fingerprinting requirements in subsection (1) of this section when there are no additional offenses of conviction from the original judgment and sentence and the defendant is in custody in a correctional facility. In such cases, the amended judgment and sentence shall reference the original judgment and sentence and the fingerprints affixed thereto.
